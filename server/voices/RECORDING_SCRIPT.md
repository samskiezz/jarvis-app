# JARVIS voice — extra recording script (read these for a more natural clone)

How to record: quiet room, phone ~20cm from your mouth, no fan/TV/music. Speak
naturally — calm and warm, the way you'd talk to mum. Pause ~1s between lines
(the pipeline auto-splits on the pauses). One long MP3 is fine. Aim for 10–20 min.
Upload at **app.projectsolar.cloud/jarvis/voiceupload** — JARVIS rebuilds the
voice automatically.

The clone copies whatever tones it hears, so include EMOTIONAL RANGE, not just flat reading.

## 1. Calm / neutral (the default assistant tone)
- "Good morning. Everything's running smoothly today."
- "I'm here. Take your time — there's no rush at all."
- "The heating's on, the doors are locked, and all is well."

## 2. Warm / reassuring (for mum — the most important)
- "It's alright. I'm right here with you, and you're safe."
- "Breathe easy. I've got everything handled."
- "You're doing wonderfully. I'm proud of you."

## 3. Short confirmations (snappy replies)
- "Done." · "Of course." · "Right away." · "Got it." · "All set." · "Consider it done."

## 4. Questions (rising intonation)
- "Would you like me to call your son?"
- "Shall I turn the lights down a little?"
- "Are you comfortable, or would you like me to adjust the temperature?"

## 5. Numbers, names, technical words (the clone struggles without these)
- "It's 3:45 in the afternoon on the 11th of June."
- "Your oxygen is at 98 percent and your heart rate is 72."
- "I've messaged Sam, and the appointment is confirmed for Tuesday at 10."

## 6. Longer natural speech (rhythm + breath)
- "Let me walk you through what's happening. The cameras are live, your medication
  reminder is set for eight, and if anything changes I'll let you know straight away.
  There's nothing for you to worry about — I'm watching over everything."

## 7. Gentle / quiet (soft register)
- (softly) "Goodnight. Rest now. I'll be right here if you need me."
- (softly) "Shhh, it's okay. I'm not going anywhere."

## 8. Light warmth / a small smile in the voice
- "Well now, that's a lovely bit of news."
- "There we are — easy as that."

Record each section. More clean variety = a warmer, more believable voice.
