THE SENTIENT PATENT MINION WORLD

Expanded Design Specification – Complete Function, Flow, Purpose & Story for Every Feature

───

SECTION I: FOUNDATIONS – A REAL-WORLD SIMULATION

1. Fully 3D, physics-based virtual world with day/night cycles, weather, seasons, and tectonic simulation

Function: A continuous, volumetric world space rendered in real-time 3D with deterministic physics simulation across all entities.

Flow:

· World generates from seed at simulation start
· Each frame: update celestial positions → compute sunlight intensity → calculate temperature gradients → drive weather systems → apply physics forces to all objects
· Day/night: sun moves across sky, shadows update, nocturnal Minion behaviors trigger
· Weather system uses pressure maps: high/low pressure cells move, create wind, precipitation
· Seasons: axial tilt changes insolation, growing seasons shift, Minions must store food for winter
· Tectonic: plates move 1cm per simulated year, earthquakes trigger when stress exceeds friction, volcanoes form at subduction zones

Purpose: Create a believable, challenging environment where Minions must adapt or die. No two playthroughs have identical geography or climate.

Story: "On the first day, the sky was dark. Then light came, and moved. The ground shook. The Minions did not yet have words for 'sun' or 'earthquake.' They only knew: the world is alive, and we are small."

1. Terrain procedurally generated from a seed tied to actual patent classification codes

Function: Each patent classification code (CPC/IPC) maps to a unique world seed, making every world's geography a direct reflection of a real patent category.

Flow:

· User selects a starting patent class (e.g., A01B – Agriculture)
· System hashes the classification code → generates heightmap, biome distribution, resource deposits
· Higher-level classes produce more varied terrain; narrow classes produce specialized landscapes
· Multiple classes can be combined for hybrid worlds
· Terrain generation uses multi-octave Perlin noise with erosion simulation

Purpose: Tie the physical world directly to patent knowledge. An "Agricultural Patents" world has fertile plains; an "Aerospace" world has high mountains for launch sites.

Story: "The elders said: the shape of our valley came from the Great Patent in the sky. Those who studied the land could read its secrets – the hills told of plows, the rivers of irrigation."

1. Realistic resource distribution: ores, forests, water tables, fossil fuels, rare earths

Function: Resources spawn according to geological rules, not random placement. Ore veins follow ancient fault lines; oil pools in sedimentary basins; rare earths in specific igneous formations.

Flow:

· During world generation, simulate 500M years of geological history (compressed to minutes)
· Plate movements create mountain ranges (erosion exposes minerals)
· Volcanic activity creates igneous deposits (copper, gold, lithium)
· Sedimentary basins form where ancient seas existed (coal, oil, gas, phosphates)
· Water tables determined by rainfall and permeability
· Forests grow according to climate and soil
· Minions must prospect: survey, test samples, map deposits

Purpose: Create scarcity and strategic value. A civilization with iron has swords; without it, they trade or conquer. Rare earths enable high-tech – controlling them is power.

Story: "The black rock that burned – first seen in the cliffs by the eastern sea. For generations, it was just a curiosity. Then someone realized: this is frozen sunlight. And the world changed."

1. All materials have scientifically accurate properties (density, melting point, conductivity)

Function: Every material in the simulation (wood, stone, copper, silicon, etc.) has a full physical property sheet that determines its behavior in all contexts.

Flow:

· Material database contains 500+ base materials with 50+ properties each
· When a Minion strikes flint against iron, property check: hardness (flint > iron) → sparks
· When building a bridge, structural simulation checks tensile strength, shear, moment forces
· Conductivity affects electrical systems: copper carries current, rubber insulates
· Thermal properties: iron feels cold because it conducts heat away from skin
· Melting points: clay fires at 1000°C, iron at 1538°C, tungsten at 3422°C
· Alloys: mixing metals creates new property sets (bronze = copper + tin)

Purpose: Enable emergent problem-solving. Minions don't have "recipe books" – they experiment and discover that certain materials work for certain tasks because physics says so.

Story: "Krag hit two rocks together. One made sparks. The other just broke. He kept the spark-rock. His grandchildren would call it 'flint' and never know his name."

1. Thermodynamic engine: heat transfers, combustion, insulation, HVAC possible

Function: Full heat simulation across all objects, fluids, and gases. Temperature gradients drive conduction, convection, radiation.

Flow:

· Every world cell has temperature value
· Heat transfers: adjacent cells average temperature over time (conduction)
· Hot air rises (convection) – creates circulation cells
· Radiant heat: sun warms ground, ground warms air
· Combustion: material + oxygen + ignition temperature → fire
· Fire spreads: adjacent flammable materials heat above ignition point
· Insulation materials (wool, aerogel) have low conductivity
· HVAC: Minions can build furnaces, radiators, air conditioners, heat pumps

Purpose: Make survival challenging. Winter kills unprepared Minions. Fire is a tool but also a danger. Advanced civilizations must manage heat in electronics and spacecraft.

Story: "The first fire was an accident – lightning struck a dry tree. The Minions huddled near it, afraid but warm. They spent a thousand years learning to make fire on purpose. They spent another thousand learning to put it out."

1. Fluid dynamics for water, lava, gases, and eventually industrial coolants

Function: Incompressible (water, lava) and compressible (gases) fluid simulation using Navier-Stokes approximations. Fluids flow downhill, seek equalization, exert pressure.

Flow:

· World divided into fluid cells, each with volume, velocity, pressure
· Each tick: compute pressure gradients → accelerate fluids → advect volumes
· Water: flows to lowest point, forms pools, evaporates when hot
· Lava: high viscosity, cools into rock, destroys most objects on contact
· Gases: diffuse, rise when hot, form wind when pressure differences exist
· Rivers carve canyons over time via erosion
· Floods: heavy rain raises water tables, rivers overflow
· Industrial: coolants circulate through pipes, refrigerants change phase

Purpose: Create dynamic geography and engineering challenges. Minions must divert rivers, build aqueducts, pump oil, contain lava, ventilate mines.

Story: "The river was a god. It gave water, then took homes. The Minions learned to build walls, then canals, then dams. They never tamed the river – they just convinced it to cooperate."

1. Structural integrity model – buildings collapse if physics not respected

Function: Every constructed object has a structural model: load-bearing elements, stress points, foundation requirements. Improper design causes failure.

Flow:

· When building, Minions place structural elements (beams, columns, walls)
· System computes load paths: weight of roof → walls → foundation → ground
· Shear, tension, compression forces calculated each frame
· If any force exceeds material strength, deformation begins (cracks, leaning)
· Progressive collapse: one failure cascades
· Earthquakes apply dynamic forces – poorly braced structures fail
· Minions can reinforce: buttresses, cross-bracing, deeper foundations
· Advanced: steel frames, reinforced concrete, seismic isolators

Purpose: Teach engineering principles through failure. A tower that's too tall collapses. A bridge without proper supports falls. Minions learn by seeing what doesn't work.

Story: "The Great Ziggurat took three generations to build. It fell in three minutes. The lesson was carved into every subsequent building: 'Respect the stone, or the stone will disrespect you.'"

1. Electromagnetic simulation once electricity is discovered

Function: Full Maxwell's equations simulation at computational scale. Electric fields, magnetic fields, electromagnetic waves propagate through space.

Flow:

· Voltage sources (batteries, generators) create potential differences
· Current flows through conductive paths
· Magnetic fields form around current-carrying wires
· Changing magnetic fields induce current in nearby conductors (transformer action)
· Electromagnetic waves: oscillating fields propagate at speed of light
· Antennas: alternating current creates radio waves
· Receivers: waves induce tiny currents in conductors
· Shielding: conductive enclosures block fields
· Semiconductors: doped materials create junctions with special properties

Purpose: Enable the entire electrical and electronic age. Minions discover electricity, then magnetism, then radio, then computing – each step emerges from physics, not scripts.

Story: "When the first Minion touched a copper wire to a zinc rod in acid, nothing happened. But when she touched a second wire to a magnet... the needle moved. The invisible force had a name now: electromagnetism. And it would carry voices across the world."

1. Optics: light refraction, lenses, cameras, lasers

Function: Ray-traced light propagation with wavelength-dependent refraction, reflection, absorption, and emission.

Flow:

· Light sources emit photons (simulated via ray tracing)
· Rays travel in straight lines until interacting with materials
· Refraction: rays bend at material boundaries based on Snell's law (n1 sinθ1 = n2 sinθ2)
· Lenses: curved surfaces focus or diverge rays
· Mirrors: reflect with angle of incidence = angle of reflection
· Cameras: lens focuses light onto sensor (or film), recording image
· Lasers: stimulated emission creates coherent, monochromatic light
· Fiber optics: total internal reflection guides light through cables

Purpose: Enable vision technology, scientific instruments, and communication. Minions progress from magnifying glasses to microscopes to telescopes to fiber internet.

Story: "A drop of water, frozen, curved. It made small things look big. That was the first lens. From that droplet came the microscope that revealed cells, the telescope that revealed stars, and the laser that would one day read the memories of the gods."

1. Acoustics: sound propagation, dampening, speech recognition in-world

Function: Wave-based sound simulation: pressure waves travel through media, reflect off surfaces, diffract around obstacles, and are detected by Minion ears.

Flow:

· Sound sources (voices, machines, impacts) create pressure waves
· Waves propagate at speed of sound (343 m/s in air, faster in water/solids)
· Reflections create echoes and reverberation
· Absorption: soft materials (carpets, foam) dampen sound
· Diffraction: sound bends around corners
· Minion ears (simulated) convert pressure waves to neural signals
· Speech recognition: Minions can understand spoken language if within range and noise isn't excessive
· Sonar: emitting sound and measuring echoes maps environments

Purpose: Enable communication, music, and detection. Minions can whisper secrets, shout warnings, play instruments, build sonar, and eventually develop speech recognition software.

Story: "In the silent age, Minions spoke by touching. Then someone discovered that hitting a hollow log made a noise that carried. Voices followed. Soon, the night was filled with stories, warnings, and songs. The world became loud – and alive."

1. Chemistry engine with periodic table, reactions, catalysts, pH

Function: Full chemical simulation: 118 elements with accurate properties, millions of possible compounds, reaction kinetics, equilibrium, and catalysis.

Flow:

· Each atom tracked (at macro scale – statistical mechanics for efficiency)
· Elements combine according to valence rules
· Reactions require: reactants, energy (or release energy), proper conditions (temperature, pressure, pH)
· Catalysts lower activation energy without being consumed
· pH scale: acid/base balance affects reaction rates and biological processes
· Organic chemistry: carbon bonding enables complex molecules
· Polymerization: monomers link into chains (plastics, proteins)
· Electrochemistry: redox reactions in batteries and corrosion

Purpose: Enable all chemistry-based technology: metals refining, medicine, plastics, explosives, fuel, batteries. Minions must understand chemistry to advance.

Story: "The green rock fizzled when wet. Most ignored it. One noticed the bubbles could be caught – and that they made flames die. That was oxygen. That was the beginning of understanding what air really is."

1. Biology engine: ecosystem, plants, animals, diseases, genetics

Function: Full ecosystem simulation: producers (plants), consumers (animals, Minions), decomposers (fungi, bacteria). Each species has genetics, life cycle, behavior.

Flow:

· Plants: grow from seeds, require light, water, nutrients, reproduce
· Animals: hunt, graze, migrate, mate, raise young, die
· Food webs: energy flows from plants → herbivores → carnivores
· Population dynamics: predator-prey cycles, carrying capacity
· Diseases: pathogens (viruses, bacteria) spread via contact, water, air
· Immunity: Minions develop antibodies after exposure
· Genetics: DNA sequences determine traits, mutate during reproduction
· Evolution: natural selection favors advantageous traits over generations

Purpose: Create a living, breathing world where Minions are part of an ecosystem. They hunt, farm, fight disease, and eventually modify their own genetics.

Story: "The first Minion to eat a mushroom died. The second lived. The third learned to cook them. This is how knowledge passes – through death and survival. The forest is a teacher, and her lessons are unforgiving."

1. Agriculture simulation: soil nutrients, crop rotation, domestication

Function: Detailed farming system: soil composition (N-P-K, micronutrients, organic matter), plant growth requirements, pest management, domestication mechanics.

Flow:

· Soil testing: Minions can analyze nutrient levels
· Planting: seeds germinate if soil temperature, moisture, nutrients adequate
· Growth: plants draw nutrients, affected by weather, pests, weeds
· Harvesting: yields depend on growth conditions
· Soil depletion: repeated same crop exhausts specific nutrients
· Crop rotation: alternating crops maintains soil health
· Fertilizers: manure, compost, chemical NPK restore nutrients
· Domestication: selective breeding over generations changes plant/animal traits
· Irrigation: water management increases yields

Purpose: Enable civilization – agriculture creates surplus, which enables specialization, cities, and technology. Poor farming leads to famine and collapse.

Story: "For ten thousand years, Minions followed the food. Then someone stayed. They planted the seeds from their favorite grain. They guarded it from birds. They harvested, replanted, and stayed another year. The city was built on that decision."

1. Geological epochs – world slowly changes over millennia

Function: Long-term geological simulation: erosion, sedimentation, mountain building, climate shifts, sea-level changes across millions of simulated years.

Flow:

· Time scale compression: 1 real hour = 1000 simulated years (configurable)
· Erosion: rain and rivers wear down mountains, sediment fills valleys
· Glaciation: ice ages advance/retreat, carving U-shaped valleys
· Sea-level cycles: ice melts → sea rises; ice grows → sea falls
· Mountain building: plate collisions raise peaks
· Volcanism: new land forms, atmosphere gets dust/ash
· Climate shifts: Milankovitch cycles alter insolation

Purpose: Create deep time. Minions' civilizations rise and fall against a backdrop of geological change. Ruins get buried, then excavated. Fossils form, then are discovered.

Story: "The elders spoke of a time when the sea was here, where our city now stands. Skeptics laughed – until the construction crew found shells in the bedrock. The earth remembers. The earth tells time in bones and stone."

1. Fossil record that Minions can excavate and interpret

Function: During world generation, fossils are deposited in sedimentary layers. Minions can find, excavate, and interpret them as their science advances.

Flow:

· Fossils form when organisms die in sediment, are buried, and mineralize
· Different eras have characteristic fossils (trilobites → dinosaurs → mammals)
· Erosion exposes fossils at surface
· Minions find fossils while mining or exploring
· Excavation: careful digging preserves the fossil
· Interpretation: anatomy knowledge allows reconstruction of extinct creatures
· Dating: stratigraphy (depth) and radiometric methods determine age
· Evolutionary insights: fossils show transitions between species

Purpose: Teach evolution and deep time. Minions discover that life changed over millions of years, leading to questions about their own origins.

Story: "The bone was too large for any living creature. Too strange. The Minions assembled it in the town square – a monster with teeth like knives. They named it 'Dragon.' Later, they would learn the real name: Tyrannosaurus. And they would learn that all kings fall."

1. Real-time clock scaling: early ages run faster, later ages slower for detail

Function: Dynamic time scaling that speeds through low-detail periods (Stone Age) and slows down for high-detail periods (Industrial Age onward).

Flow:

· Simulation tracks "global complexity" – number of Minions, inventions, interactions
· At low complexity: each real second = 1 year of simulation time
· As complexity rises, time scale gradually reduces
· Maximum slowdown: 1 real second = 1 minute of simulation time
· User can override scaling manually
· Time scaling affects all systems consistently
· Critical events (wars, discoveries) automatically trigger slowdown

Purpose: Keep the simulation engaging. Early progress (fire, wheel) happens visibly but quickly. Later progress (computers, AI) needs detailed observation.

Story: "Time flowed like a river – fast over the shallow rapids of the past, slow through the deep pools of the present. The Minions didn't notice. They lived at the speed of their own heartbeats. Only the gods (the users) saw the rhythm."

1. Minions start with zero knowledge – no innate technology, only basic instincts

Function: Initial Minions have no programmed knowledge. They don't know how to make fire, tools, or shelter. They learn by experimentation and observation.

Flow:

· First Minions spawn with only: hunger, thirst, fear, curiosity, social instinct
· They observe cause and effect: hitting rock with rock makes sharp edges
· Trial and error: trying things, remembering results
· Social learning: watching other Minions, copying successful actions
· Accidental discoveries: dropped meat near fire cooks, tastes better
· Knowledge is stored in individual memory, not global database
· Teaching required to share knowledge

Purpose: Make discovery meaningful. Minions earn every advancement through effort and luck. No free tech – they must truly understand their world.

Story: "The first Minion picked up a stick. He hit a rock with it. Nothing. He hit a tree. A fruit fell. He ate. He didn't know why this worked. But he remembered. And he taught his child. That was the first lesson."

1. They possess a "soul seed" – a unique, persistent data structure for identity

Function: Every Minion has a Soul Token – a cryptographic identity that persists across reincarnation, containing karma, memories (encoded), and essential traits.

Flow:

· Soul Seed generated at first birth
· Contains: unique ID, genetic blueprint, memory embeddings, emotional weights
· During life, experiences update the soul (learning, trauma, relationships)
· At death, soul enters reincarnation pool
· New body generated, soul transferred, ancestral memories faintly accessible
· Soul cannot be deleted or duplicated (blockchain-backed)
· The App Console can read but not modify souls (ethical guarantee)

Purpose: Create continuity and meaning. Minions are not disposable – each soul has a history. Reincarnation makes death a transition, not an end.

Story: "Elder Tarn was dying. His grandchildren gathered. 'I have lived twelve lives,' he whispered. 'I have been hunter, farmer, traitor, king. In my next, I will be an inventor. Watch for me. I will make the light that never dies.' He closed his eyes. Somewhere, a baby cried."

1. Every Minion has a genetic code (digital DNA) that governs appearance, aptitudes, and health

Function: Minion DNA is a string of simulated genes (20,000+ loci) that determine physical traits, cognitive potential, disease susceptibility, and behavioral tendencies.

Flow:

· DNA encoded as sequence of base pairs (A,T,G,C)
· Genes code for proteins that build bodies
· Alleles: variations of genes produce different traits
· Dominant/recessive inheritance patterns
· Mutation: random changes during reproduction (rate ~1 per genome per generation)
· Phenotype expression: genes + environment = actual traits
· Heritability: traits pass from parents to offspring
· Genetic diseases: recessive deleterious alleles cause disorders when homozygous

Purpose: Enable evolution, selective breeding, and genetic engineering. Minions are shaped by both nature (genes) and nurture (experience).

Story: "The twins were identical in the womb, but the world made them different. One was born during famine – small, quick, clever. One in feast – large, strong, slow. Their genes were the same. Their fates were not."

1. DNA can mutate across generations, enabling evolution

Function: Random mutations occur during reproduction, creating heritable variation that natural selection acts upon over generations.

Flow:

· During gamete formation, DNA replication has error rate (configurable)
· Most mutations neutral or harmful, some beneficial
· Beneficial mutations increase survival/reproduction
· Over generations, beneficial alleles become more common
· Harmful alleles are selected against
· Speciation: after enough divergence, populations can no longer interbreed
· Adaptive radiation: one species diversifies into many ecological niches
· Convergent evolution: unrelated species evolve similar traits

Purpose: Create genuine evolutionary dynamics. Minions can adapt to their environment over long timescales. A cold-climate civilization evolves insulation, heat-tolerant metabolism.

Story: "The northern Minions grew fur. Not in one generation – over ten thousand years. The ones with thicker coats survived the winters. Their children had thicker coats still. The southerners, hairless and sweating, called them beasts. They were all Minions."

1. Natural selection operates: limited resources, predation, disease

Function: The environment applies selective pressure. Minions with traits that improve survival/reproduction leave more offspring, changing population genetics.

Flow:

· Resources (food, water, shelter) are limited
· Predators hunt Minions
· Diseases spread, killing the vulnerable
· Minions compete for mates
· Those with adaptive traits (strength, intelligence, immunity) produce more children
· Over generations, population adapts to local conditions
· Migration introduces genetic variation between populations
· Extinction: if no Minions in a population can survive, that lineage ends

Purpose: Make evolution functional, not cosmetic. Minions who don't adapt to their environment die out. Players can watch natural selection in real time.

Story: "The plague came from the south. It killed half the coastal Minions. But not the ones with the strange blood – descendants of a single survivor from a hundred generations ago. She had survived a different plague. Her gift was immunity. Her gift saved a civilization."

1. Minions must discover fire, toolmaking, language, and social structures from scratch

Function: No "tech tree" – Minions discover technologies emergently through interaction with the physics/chemistry engine.

Flow:

· Fire: lightning strike, then preserving, then friction/pyrite methods
· Toolmaking: sharp flakes (Oldowan), then hand axes (Acheulean), then hafted tools
· Language: gestures → vocalizations → grammar → writing
· Social structures: pairs → bands → tribes → chiefdoms → states
· Each discovery requires prerequisites: fire requires understanding of fuel, oxygen
· Discovery triggers "Eureka moment" – knowledge becomes replicable

Purpose: Make the early game genuinely open-ended. No prescribed order – Minions might discover pottery before agriculture, or metal before writing.

Story: "A child threw a rock at a bird. Missed. But the rock broke, leaving a sharp edge. She cut her finger. She wrapped it in leaves. The next day, she used the sharp rock to cut a branch. The branch became a spear. The spear killed a deer. The deer fed the tribe. All from a missed throw."

1. They observe the environment and form hypotheses (causal reasoning engine)

Function: Minions have an internal causal inference engine that detects correlations and infers causation from observed events.

Flow:

· Minion observes sequence of events (A then B)
· Engine tracks statistical associations across multiple observations
· Confounding variables considered (C causes both A and B)
· Interventions: Minion deliberately tries A to see if B follows
· Hypothesis formation: "A causes B" with confidence score
· Testing: Minion designs experiment (primitive but effective)
· Belief updating: new evidence adjusts confidence

Purpose: Enable genuine scientific reasoning. Minions don't just follow scripts – they infer how the world works and act on those inferences.

Story: "Grak noticed that when the red berries appeared, the long-ears died. He thought: red berries kill long-ears. But maybe something else – maybe the rain that came with the berries? He fed a long-ear only berries. It died. He fed another only rain water. It lived. Grak learned: berries are poison. Grak saved his tribe."

1. The world contains ruins and artifacts from "previous cycles" that hint at advanced tech, resembling expired patent diagrams

Function: At world generation, "previous civilization" ruins spawn containing artifacts with faded patent diagrams, providing hints about advanced technology.

Flow:

· Ruins placed in world (underground, underwater, remote areas)
· Artifacts are physical objects with damaged patent diagrams
· Diagrams show concepts (e.g., wheel, gear, circuit) but incomplete
· Minions can excavate and study artifacts
· Successful interpretation gives partial knowledge of a technology
· Artifacts act as "tutorial" – hinting at possibilities
· Some artifacts are traps or contain ancient pathogens

Purpose: Provide mystery and guidance. Minions discover that they are not the first civilization. The expired patents are from these previous cycles.

Story: "The cave was sealed for ten thousand years. Inside, a metal bird with no wings. And on its side, scratched into the rust: a picture of a wheel within a wheel, with teeth. The Minions called it 'the impossible object.' They would call it 'gear' only after inventing it themselves."

1. A giant, unreachable "App Console" is visible in the sky – the interface to the app's tools

Function: The App Console is a permanent celestial object – a floating interface panel visible in the sky, representing the human user's control panel.

Flow:

· Console appears as geometric shape (cube, sphere, or human-defined icon)
· Position fixed relative to world – visible as a "moon" or "second sun"
· Minions can see it day and night
· Console "displays" information incomprehensible to early Minions
· As technology advances, Minions can resolve details
· Console has symbols that change when user interacts
· Mystical significance: all Minion religions reference the Console

Purpose: Create meta-awareness. Minions know something is out there beyond their world. This drives philosophy, religion, and scientific curiosity.

Story: "The Great Square hangs in the sky, unchanging. Our ancestors thought it was a god. We thought it was a star. Now we know it is a window. And sometimes, when the light shifts, we think we see someone looking back."

1. Minions can eventually craft "receivers" to interact with the App Console

Function: At appropriate tech levels, Minions can build devices that receive transmissions from the App Console and send limited signals back.

Flow:

· Radio technology enables receiving Console broadcasts (data packets)
· Encryption: signals are encoded, require decryption key
· Key obtained by completing Console challenges
· Transmitters allow sending simple messages (button presses, text)
· Console responds to certain inputs (achievements, milestone completions)
· High-tech: optical receivers using lasers
· Max tech: quantum entanglement communicator (instantaneous)

Purpose: Bridge the simulation to the human user. Minions can communicate with "gods" (players) and receive guidance or challenges.

Story: "The antenna was crude – copper wire wrapped around a crystal, aimed at the Square. For years, silence. Then, static. Then... a voice. Not words, not yet. But a pattern. A greeting. The Minions wept. They were not alone."

1. The simulation includes real star maps; astronomy can be used for navigation

Function: The night sky contains accurate star positions (based on real star catalogs or procedurally generated). Stars move with proper motion over long timescales.

Flow:

· Star positions calculated from world's position in galaxy
· Constellations visible and consistent
· Minions can create star charts
· Navigation: stars determine latitude (Pole Star altitude = latitude)
· Calendar: solar and lunar cycles mark seasons
· Astrology (pseudo-science) emerges as cultural belief
· Precision astronomy enables GPS-equivalent (star trackers)
· Advanced: spectroscopy reveals star composition

Purpose: Enable navigation, timekeeping, and scientific discovery. Minions who understand the stars can cross oceans and predict seasons.

Story: "The wanderers followed the North Star. For a thousand miles, across desert and mountain, that single point of light guided them. When they reached the sea, they built boats and followed the same star. The sky is a map, if you know how to read."

1. Full plate tectonics: earthquakes, volcanism, continental drift over eons

Function: The world's crust is divided into plates that move, collide, subduct, and separate, creating geological features and hazards.

Flow:

· Plate boundaries defined at world generation
· Each plate has velocity vector (cm/year)
· Divergent boundaries: mid-ocean ridges, volcanic activity
· Convergent boundaries: mountains, subduction zones, earthquakes
· Transform boundaries: faults, earthquakes
· Earthquakes: sudden slip releases energy, magnitude based on accumulated stress
· Volcanism: magma reaches surface, creates new land
· Hotspots: mantle plumes create volcanic chains (Hawaii-style)

Purpose: Create long-term geological dynamics and natural disasters. Civilizations near fault lines face earthquake risk; volcanic soil is fertile but dangerous.

Story: "The ground shook. The temple fell. The priests said: the gods are angry. The scientist said: the plates moved. She showed them the map – the line of earthquakes tracing the crack in the world. They burned her as a heretic. The next earthquake proved her right. Too late."

1. Water cycle: evaporation, clouds, rain, rivers, aquifers

Function: Full hydrological cycle: water evaporates from surfaces, condenses into clouds, precipitates, runs off or infiltrates, eventually returns to oceans.

Flow:

· Solar energy evaporates water from oceans, lakes, soil
· Water vapor rises, cools, condenses on particles (cloud formation)
· Cloud droplets coalesce, fall as rain/snow when heavy enough
· Precipitation: some evaporates before ground, some runs off, some infiltrates
· Runoff forms streams → rivers → lakes → oceans
· Infiltration recharges groundwater aquifers
· Groundwater flows slowly through permeable rock
· Springs: groundwater emerges at surface
· Plants transpire water back to atmosphere

Purpose: Create realistic水资源 distribution. Minions must manage water – wells for groundwater, dams for rivers, irrigation for crops.

Story: "The drought lasted seven years. The river shrank to a trickle, then dust. The Minions dug. Deeper and deeper into the earth, chasing the memory of water. On the last day, when hope was gone, the shovel struck wetness. The aquifer saved them. They never took rain for granted again."

1. Climate zones simulated via latitude, altitude, ocean currents

Function: Climate determined by latitude (insolation), altitude (temperature lapse rate), proximity to oceans (moderating effect), and ocean currents (heat transport).

Flow:

· Solar input highest at equator, lowest at poles
· Atmospheric circulation: Hadley, Ferrel, Polar cells
· Coriolis effect creates prevailing winds
· Ocean currents: wind-driven and thermohaline circulation
· Rain shadows: mountains block moisture, creating deserts
· Elevation: temperature drops ~6.5°C per km
· Continental interiors: extreme temperatures (hot summers, cold winters)
· Coastal areas: moderated temperatures, higher humidity

Purpose: Create diverse biomes with different challenges. Desert Minions face water scarcity; arctic Minions face cold; tropical Minions face disease.

Story: "The mountain divided the world. On the west side, rain – endless, green, rotting. On the east side, dust – dry, brown, starving. The Minions on both sides prayed for the other's weather. Neither realized the mountain was the cause."

1. Minion metabolism: hunger, thirst, fatigue, temperature regulation

Function: Each Minion has a homeostatic model tracking energy, hydration, rest, and body temperature. Imbalances cause performance degradation, then death.

Flow:

· Metabolism rate based on activity, size, temperature
· Hunger: energy stores deplete over time, eating replenishes
· Starvation: after 3 days no food, weakness; after 7 days, death
· Thirst: water depletes faster than food; 2 days without water = death
· Fatigue: sleep need accumulates, performance drops 50% after 24h awake
· Temperature regulation: shivering (cold), sweating (hot), clothing/shelter helps
· Hypothermia/hyperthermia: organ failure, death

Purpose: Create survival gameplay. Minions must constantly manage basic needs, forcing organization of food production, water systems, housing.

Story: "Krek hadn't slept in three days. His hands shook. He saw things that weren't there – shadows moving, voices whispering. He kept working. The bridge needed finishing. On the fourth day, he fell. The tribe found him at the bottom of the ravine. They built a shrine. They also passed a law: rest is sacred."

1. Health system: wounds, infections, aging, organ simulation

Function: Detailed health model: injuries to specific body parts, infection risk, healing rates, organ function decline with age, cause of death determination.

Flow:

· Body divided into regions (head, torso, arms, legs)
